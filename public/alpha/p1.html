Overview

Your first task will be to "decrypt" a list of calculations using a key. The key will describe what sequence of characters correspond to which number or symbol. You will have to calculate each encrypted line of the input.

Input

Key

A key consisting of what symbols represent what numbers and operations.

Note: While these examples use the key at the top, your input will contain a randomly generated key.

0: bcad
1: bacd
2: adbc
...
+: cadb
-: cdba

Expressions

A list of 1000 encoded expressions to calculate. Multiple digits in a single number are possible, as well.

adbccadbbacd            //Translates to 2 + 1
bacdbcadadbccdbaadbc    //Translates to 102 - 2

Output

The output of the program should be a single integer that is the sum of every single operation. Given the two expressions and key shown previously, the output would be 103.

adbccadbbacd            //2 + 1 = 3
bacdbcadadbccdbaadbc    //102 - 2 = 100
3 + 100 = 103

Constants/Constraints

  • Every line will be a single expression
  • Every expression will contain exactly two numbers separated by a single operation
  • The numbers may not be the same length of digits
  • The numbers will always be non-negative
  • The results may be negative or zero
