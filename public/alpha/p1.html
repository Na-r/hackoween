Overview

Your first task will be to "decrypt" a list of calculations using a key. The key will describe what strings translate to which number or symbol. You will have to read in the key, and use it to decipher each encrypted line of the input.

Input

Key

A key consisting of what symbols represent what numbers and operations.

Note: While the following examples use this key, your input will contain a randomly generated key.

0: bcad
1: bacd
2: adbc
...
+: cadb
-: cdba

Expressions

A list of 1000 encoded expressions to calculate. Multiple digits in a single number are possible, as well.

adbccadbbacd            //Translates to 2 + 1
bacdbcadadbccdbaadbc    //Translates to 102 - 2
adbccdbabacdadbc        //Translates to 2 - 12

Output

The output of the program should be a single integer that is the sum of every single operation. Given the expressions on the right and the key shown previously, the output would be 93.

adbccadbbacd            //2 + 1 = 3
bacdbcadadbccdbaadbc    //102 - 2 = 100
adbccdbabacdadbc        //2 - 12 = -10
3 + 100 + (-10) = 93

Constants/Constraints

  • Every input will begin with the key
  • After the key and an empty line, every line will be a single expression
  • Every expression will contain exactly two numbers separated by a single operation
  • The numbers may not be the same length of digits
  • The numbers will always be non-negative
  • The results may be negative or zero
